# Frequently Asked Questions for Pakistani Founders

## Company Registration

### Q1: What's the quickest and cheapest way to start a business in Pakistan?
**A:** Sole proprietorship with NTN registration. It's free and takes 1-5 days online via [FBR IRIS](https://iris.fbr.gov.pk). No SECP registration needed. Best for testing ideas with zero cost.

### Q2: I'm a solo founder. Should I register an SMC or stay as sole proprietor?
**A:** Start sole proprietor if low-risk (consulting, writing). Switch to SMC (PKR 15-25K cost) when you have assets to protect or need investor credibility. SMC = personal asset protection.

### Q3: How long does it take to register a private limited company?
**A:** 2-3 working days if documents are complete and ready. With consultant help: 5-7 days including document preparation. Total cost: PKR 20-50K.

### Q4: Can I change business structure later (e.g., sole proprietor → SMC)?
**A:** Yes. You can dissolve sole proprietorship and register SMC. Current registrations/licenses may need updating. Consult tax advisor for implications.

### Q5: What's the minimum number of people needed for each structure?
**A:** 
- Sole Proprietorship: 1 person
- SMC: 1 person
- Private Limited: 2 directors, 2 shareholders minimum
- Partnership: 2-20 partners

## Tax Registration

### Q6: Is NTN registration mandatory for freelancers?
**A:** Yes. Needed for business bank accounts. Also required if any income source is in Pakistan. Registration is free at [FBR IRIS](https://iris.fbr.gov.pk).

### Q7: What's the difference between NTN and filer status?
**A:** NTN = tax registration number (free, automatic). Filer status = registered with FBR after filing first annual return. You become a filer by filing on time.

### Q8: Do I need STRN if my business is just starting?
**A:** Only if your annual turnover will exceed PKR 10M (retailers/wholesalers) or any amount (manufacturers/importers). If below threshold, just NTN is enough initially.

### Q9: What happens if I don't file my tax return on time?
**A:** 
- Penalties and interest on unpaid tax
- Possible removal from filer list
- Difficulty getting credit/loans
- Compliance issues for future business

### Q10: Can I file income tax as freelancer without STRN?
**A:** Yes. STRN is only for sales tax (collecting 18% from customers). Income tax filing works with just NTN.

## Freelancer-Specific

### Q11: What tax rate do freelancers pay on foreign income?
**A:** 
- Up to PKR 5M: 0.25% final tax
- PKR 5-10M: 0.5% final tax
- Above PKR 10M: 1% final tax
Rates apply only to export income (work for foreign clients).

### Q12: Can I invoice local clients while registered as exporter?
**A:** Yes. Local income is taxed separately under normal income tax slabs (5-35%). Export income = final tax rates above. Keep both separate in records.

### Q13: By what date must I file my annual return?
**A:** September 30 every year. File by this date to avoid penalties. If missed, you lose filer status and face daily penalties.

### Q14: Do I need to maintain invoices for 5+ years?
**A:** Yes. FBR can audit up to 5 years back. Keep invoices, receipts, bank statements, and proof of payments. Digital copies are acceptable.

### Q15: What expenses can I deduct from freelance income?
**A:** Professional expenses: software subscriptions, internet, office rent, equipment, professional development, bank charges. Keep receipts for all deductions.

## Compliance & Ongoing

### Q16: What are annual compliance requirements for SMC?
**A:** 
- File annual return to FBR (by Sept 30)
- Submit annual accounts to SECP (within 4 months of year-end)
- Pass annual SECP compliance fee
- Maintain 5-year record books

### Q17: What if my company has zero income/profit?
**A:** You still must file return (even with zero income). Shows due diligence. Sole proprietors in loss can offset against other income.

### Q18: How often is sales tax return filed?
**A:** Monthly by the 15th of following month. You can claim input tax on purchases. Missing deadline = penalties.

### Q19: Can a foreigner register a company in Pakistan?
**A:** Foreign resident: Can be shareholder/director but needs valid visa/CNIC equivalent. SECP and FBR have specific requirements. Consult SECP first.

### Q20: What documents do I need to open a business bank account?
**A:** 
- NTN certificate
- CNIC
- Address proof (utility bill)
- Business registration documents (if company)
- Authorized signatory form
