# Private Limited Company Registration in Pakistan

## Summary
A private limited company is a separate legal entity with limited liability. Shareholders' personal assets are protected. Ideal for startups seeking investment and professional growth.

## Eligibility
- Minimum 2 shareholders required
- Minimum 2 directors required
- Can have up to 50 members (traditionally)
- All directors must have valid CNIC

## Required Information
- Valid CNICs of all directors and shareholders
- Registered office address (landed property preferred)
- Memorandum of Association (MoA)
- Articles of Association (AoA)
- Proposed company name (3 options for search)
- Proof of office address ownership/lease
- Utility bill of office address

## Registration Steps
1. **Name Search:** [SECP eServices](https://eservices.secp.gov.pk) → Name Availability Check (~PKR 200)
2. **Digital Signatures:** Obtain digital signatures for all directors
3. **Document Preparation:** Draft MoA and AoA (templates available on SECP)
4. **Form 1 Filing:** File incorporation form via eServices with all documents
5. **Fee Payment:** Pay incorporation fee (typically PKR 1,500-20,000 based on capital)
6. **Processing:** SECP processes within 2-3 working days if complete
7. **Certificate:** Receive Certificate of Incorporation via email

## Estimated Timeline
- **With all documents ready:** 2-3 working days
- **If documents need correction:** 5-7 working days
- **With professional consultant:** 1-2 weeks (including document prep)

## Fees
- Name Reservation: ~PKR 200
- Incorporation Fee: PKR 1,500 (up to PKR 100,000 capital) to PKR 20,000+ for larger capital
- Professional Fees: PKR 10,000-25,000 (if using consultant)
- Digital Signature Cost: PKR 2,500-5,000 per director

## Annual Compliance
- Annual return to SECP (within 4 months of year-end)
- Directors meeting minutes
- Audited accounts (if turnover > PKR 30 million)
- Tax return to FBR
- Payment of annual fee to SECP

## Notes for Founders
- Limited liability protects personal assets from business debt
- Better credibility with banks, investors, and clients
- Can issue shares for investment
- More complex compliance than sole proprietorship
- Ideal if planning to raise external funding
- Corporate structure appeals to international clients

Source: [SECP eServices](https://eservices.secp.gov.pk) | [Companies Act 2017](https://secp.gov.pk) | [SECP Companies Registration](https://secp.gov.pk/Document/)
