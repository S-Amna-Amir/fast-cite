# Sole Proprietorship in Pakistan

## Summary
A sole proprietorship is not a separate company. You and your business are legally the same. No SECP registration required. Simplest structure to start with minimal cost and paperwork.

## Typical Registration/Compliance Path

### Getting Started
1. **Get NTN:** Register free at [FBR IRIS](https://iris.fbr.gov.pk) - takes 1-5 days
2. **That's it:** No other registration needed for sole proprietorship
3. **Name Registration (Optional):** You can register business name with SECP for trademark-like protection

### Annual Compliance
- File income tax return by September 30
- Maintain business records for 5 years
- If turnover exceeds PKR 10M and sales occur, consider STRN

## Tax and Banking Considerations

### Tax Filing
- **Income Tax:** File annual income tax return as individual
- **Tax Rates:** Progressive rates (5% to 35% depending on income)
- **Deductions:** Can deduct legitimate business expenses
- **Record Keeping:** Maintain books/records for audit trail
- **Filer Status:** Filing return makes you registered filer

### Opening Bank Account
- Most banks accept NTN for business account
- Some require "Sole Proprietorship Declaration"
- Account title example: "[Your Name] Trading as [Business Name]"
- Utility bill for address proof usually required

### Sales Tax
- If annual sales exceed PKR 10M, STRN registration becomes mandatory
- Otherwise, you can operate on simple income tax basis

## Advantages
- **Zero Registration Cost** (except optional NTN - which is free)
- **Minimal Paperwork:** Simple record keeping
- **Quick to Start:** Begin business in days
- **Full Control:** All decisions are yours
- **Flexible:** Easy to close or change business direction

## Disadvantages
- **Unlimited Liability:** Personal assets exposed if sued or in debt
- **Bank Perception:** Some institutions see as less formal
- **Personal Tax Liability:** Business debt becomes personal debt
- **Harder to Sell:** No separate entity to sell
- **Investment Difficulty:** Harder to take investor capital

## When Sole Proprietorship Works Well
- Initial testing phase (validate idea before bigger investment)
- Low-risk services (consulting, writing, design work)
- No significant assets to protect
- Local, small-scale business
- Sole trader intention

## When to Upgrade to SMC
- Business becomes profitable and accumulates assets
- Risk increases (operating equipment, staff, liability)
- Considering future investment
- Improving professional credibility needed
- Planning to bring in partners

## Key Points
- No SECP company registration required
- NTN registration is free and online
- Annual tax return filing mandatory if earning income
- Simple but personally risky structure
- Perfect for testing business ideas
