# Single Member Company (SMC) Registration

## Summary
An SMC is a private company with only one shareholder (you). It combines the simplicity of sole proprietorship with the legal protection of a company structure. Perfect for solo founders who want limited liability.

## Eligibility
- Only 1 shareholder allowed
- Only 1 director required
- Must be Pakistani national with valid CNIC
- No age restriction but must be of sound mind

## Comparison: SMC vs Sole Proprietorship
| Aspect | SMC | Sole Proprietorship |
|---|---|---|
| Legal Entity | Separate company | Part of owner |
| Liability | Limited | Unlimited |
| Registration Cost | ~PKR 15,000-25,000 | PKR 0 (NTN only) |
| Formality | Higher | Lower |
| Bank Confidence | Better | Lower |
| Registration Authority | SECP | FBR (NTN only) |

## Required Information
- Valid CNIC of sole member
- Registered office address
- Memorandum of Association (MoA)
- Articles of Association (AoA) - SMC variant
- Utility bill of office address

## Registration Steps
1. **Name Search:** [SECP eServices](https://eservices.secp.gov.pk) → Check availability (~PKR 200)
2. **Digital Signature:** Get digital signature for the member
3. **MoA and AoA:** Use SMC templates (different from Pvt Ltd)
4. **Form 1 (SMC):** File incorporation form specifically marked as SMC
5. **Fee Payment:** Deposit registration fee (typically PKR 1,500-5,000 for SMC)
6. **Processing:** SECP approves within 2-3 working days
7. **Certificate:** Download certificate from eServices

## Estimated Timeline
- **Complete documents:** 2-3 working days
- **With consultant:** 5-7 working days including preparation

## Fees
- Name Reservation: ~PKR 200
- Incorporation Fee: PKR 1,500-3,000 (lower than Pvt Ltd)
- Digital Signature: PKR 2,500-5,000
- Professional Services: PKR 5,000-10,000 (optional)
- **Total Typical Cost: PKR 15,000-25,000**

## Annual Compliance
- Annual return to SECP
- Financial accounts
- Tax return to FBR
- Annual SECP fee

## Key Points for Solo Founders
- Personal assets protected from business liabilities
- Much cheaper than private limited company
- Simpler than multi-member company
- Can later become private limited if you take on partners
- Better for long-term business credibility
- Suitable for freelancers/consultants with risk exposure

## When to Choose SMC
- You are sole founder with significant personal assets
- You offer professional services (consulting, development, etc.)
- You plan to grow but want protection now
- You want professional structure without partnership

Source: [SECP eServices](https://eservices.secp.gov.pk) | [Companies Act 2017 - Section 42](https://secp.gov.pk)
