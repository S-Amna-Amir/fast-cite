# Partnership Firm Registration

## Summary
A partnership is a business relationship between 2-20 partners. Registered with provincial Registrar of Firms (not SECP). Partners share profits, losses, and unlimited liability.

## Eligibility
- Minimum 2 partners required
- Maximum 20 partners (traditional; some sectors have different limits)
- All partners must be individuals (companies cannot be partners in traditional partnership)
- No age restriction but must be of legal capacity
- Can be Pakistani nationals or foreign nationals

## Formation Basics

### Partnership Deed (Essential)
A partnership deed is a written agreement covering:
- Partner names and contributions
- Profit-sharing ratio
- Management responsibilities
- Withdrawal/dissolution terms
- Dispute resolution process
- Capital contributions

### Registration Process
1. **Draft Partnership Deed:** All partners must sign (notarized recommended)
2. **Locate Registrar:** Find provincial Registrar of Firms office
3. **Submit Registration Application:** Include:
   - Original partnership deed
   - CNICs of all partners
   - Office address proof
   - Published notice in newspaper
4. **Pay Registration Fee:** Varies by province (~PKR 2,000-5,000)
5. **Approval:** Typically within 7-15 business days
6. **Registration Certificate:** Issued upon approval

## Registration and Compliance

### Initial Registration
- **Where:** Provincial Registrar of Firms (not SECP)
- **Documents:** Partnership deed with signatures
- **Timeline:** 1-2 weeks typically
- **Cost:** ~PKR 5,000 (varies by province)

### Annual Compliance
- **Tax Return:** Partnership firm files consolidated return
- **Individual Returns:** Each partner also files personal return
- **Accounting Records:** Maintain books of accounts for 5 years
- **Profit/Loss Distribution:** Document how profits are divided
- **No SECP Filing:** Unlike companies, no annual SECP filing needed

## Tax Considerations

### How Partnership is Taxed
- **Firm Level:** Firm calculates total income and losses
- **Partner Level:** Each partner liable for tax on their share
- **Not Double Taxed:** Only partners pay personal tax on shares
- **Loss Carry Forward:** Partnership losses can offset partner's other income

### Tax Filing
- Each partner files individual return showing partnership income
- Partnership return filed showing total income (may or may not be separate form)
- Deductions available for partnership expenses
- Partners pay tax at their individual slab rates

### Example
- Partnership earns PKR 5 million profit
- Split 50-50 between two partners
- Each partner declares PKR 2.5 million income
- Each pays tax based on personal total income and slab

## Liability Considerations

### Unlimited Liability (Key Risk)
- **Partners Personally Liable:** Business debts are personal debts
- **Personal Assets at Risk:** Creditors can claim personal property
- **Joint and Several:** Partners liable even for others' actions
- **No Separation:** Partnership business ≠ separated personal liability

### Comparison with Private Limited
| Aspect | Partnership | Private Limited |
|---|---|---|
| Liability | Unlimited | Limited |
| Registration | Registrar of Firms | SECP |
| Partners/Members | 2-20 | 2-50+ |
| Complexity | Simple | Complex |
| Cost | ~PKR 5,000 | ~PKR 20,000+ |
| Asset Protection | None | Full |

## When Partnership Works Well
- **Professional Services:** Law, accounting, consulting firms
- **Family Businesses:** Built on trust and shared values
- **Equal Partners:** All partners involved in management
- **Simple Structure:** Low overhead preferred

## When to Consider Private Limited Instead
- **Risk Exposure:** Want to limit personal liability
- **External Investment:** Want to raise outside capital
- **Large Operations:** Growing beyond 20 partners
- **Asset Protection:** Significant assets to protect

## Key Points
- Partnership is not SECP company, no corporate veil
- All partners personally liable for firm debts
- Registration with provincial Registrar of Firms
- Simple to form (partnership deed is core document)
- No double taxation (only partners pay individual tax)
- Works best for professional service firms
